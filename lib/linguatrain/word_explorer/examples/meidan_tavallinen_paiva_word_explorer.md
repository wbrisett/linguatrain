# Finnish Morphology used in Meidän tavallinen päivä

A running log of grammatical forms as they appear in actual course dialogues. Each lesson is broken into five categories:

1. **Cases** — grouped by case, so every partitive/adessive/etc. stacks up together
2. **Compounds** — two (or more) whole words fused into one
3. **Derivations** — a suffix builds a new word/meaning from a base (e.g. place → nationality)
4. **Passive forms** — the impersonal "is done / one does" construction
5. **Irregular stems** — words that don't just add an ending to the dictionary form, but shift shape first

*(A category heading is omitted if it has no entries — see the Passive forms note below.)*

## Meidän tavallinen päivä

### Cases

**Partitive (-a/-ä)**
| Finnish form | Base word | Formation | Meaning | Where it appears |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **lasta** | lapsi | lapsi → **las-** + **-a** | child (some, after a number) | *kaksi lasta.* |

**Inessive (-ssa/-ssä)**
| Finnish form | Base word | Formation | Meaning | Where it appears |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **suihkussa** | suihku | suihku + **-ssa** | in the shower | *Käyn ensin suihkussa* |
| **töissä** | työ | työ → **töi-** (irregular) + **-ssä** | at work | *Olen töissä* |

**Illative (-Vn / -hin / -maan)**
| Finnish form | Base word | Formation | Meaning | Where it appears |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **kouluun** | koulu | koulu + **-un** | to school | *Kalle menee kouluun* |
| **nukkumaan** | nukkua | nukkua → **nukku-** + **-maan** (3rd infinitive) | to sleep (purpose) | *lapset menevät nukkumaan.* |

**Adessive (-lla/-llä)**
| Finnish form | Base word | Formation | Meaning | Where it appears |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **minulla** | minä | minä → **minu-** + **-lla** | on me → I have | *Minulla on* |

**Ablative (-lta/-ltä)**
| Finnish form | Base word | Formation | Meaning | Where it appears |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **kahdelta** | kaksi | kaksi → **kahde-** (irregular) + **-lta** | at two o'clock | *kahdelta.* |
| **yhdeksältä** | yhdeksän | yhdeksän → **yhdeksä-** + **-ltä** | at nine o'clock | *Yhdeksältä* |

### Compounds
| Compound | Parts | Literal sense | Where it appears |
|---|---|---|---|
| **herätyskello** | herätys (← herätä) + kello | alarm clock | *herätyskello soi.* |
| **aamupala** → aamupalaa | aamu + pala | breakfast | *teen keittiössä aamupalaa* |
| **lounasravintola** → lounasravintolassa | lounas + ravintola | lunch restaurant | *lounasravintolassa.* |
| **työkaveri** → työkaverin | työ + kaveri | coworker | *kolmen työkaverin kanssa.* |
| **päiväkoti** → päiväkotiin | päivä + koti | daycare | *päiväkotiin.* |
| **uimahalli** → uimahalliin | uima (← uida) + halli | swimming hall / indoor pool | *uimahalliin.* |
| **kenkäkauppa** → kenkäkauppaan | kenkä + kauppa | shoe store | *He menevät kenkäkauppaan,* |

### Derivations
| Form | Base + suffix | Function | Where it appears |
|---|---|---|---|
| **suomalainen** | suomi → **suoma-** (i → a) + **-lainen** | demonym — names a person or thing belonging to a place | *suomalainen mies* |

*(No Passive forms in this lesson — the dialogue doesn't use the passive present)*

### Irregular stems
| Word | Nominative | Case stem | Why irregular |
|---|---|---|---|
| minä | minä | **minu-** | Personal pronoun stem doesn't match the nominative — every case builds off *minu-*, not *minä-*. |
| työ | työ | **töi-** | The locative/directional forms used for "at work" / "to work" (*töissä*, *töihin*) shift the stem to *töi-*, a change the Vocabulary pack lists as forms to memorize directly rather than derive. |
| kaksi | kaksi | **kahde-** | The number "two" takes its own case stem, *kahde-*, not shared with most nouns. Several small numbers (*yksi → yhde-*, *kaksi → kahde-*, *viisi → viide-*) each need to be learned individually. |

**Notes:**
- *Minulla on* ("on me is") is how Finnish expresses possession — there is no verb "to have."
- *Käydä suihkussa* is a fixed expression: literally "to go in the shower," idiomatically "to take a shower."
- *Olen töissä* is a fixed idiom for "I work" / "I'm at work." Työ's locative forms don't follow a regular pattern and are best learned as set phrases, the same way *minulla* and *minulle* are learned as forms of *minä* rather than derived on the fly.
- Clock time — "at two o'clock," "at nine o'clock" — uses the Ablative case (*kahdelta*, *yhdeksältä*), the same case pattern introduced with *kello 7.15* in this lesson's earlier sentences.
- Compounds are written as one word, with only the final part taking case endings: *aamupala***a**, *lounasravintola***ssa**, *kenkäkauppa***an**.
- *Suomalainen* and *ruotsalainen* share the same *-lainen* demonym pattern, with an i → a vowel shift before the suffix for country names ending in *-i*.
- *Mennä* + the third infinitive Illative (*nukkumaan*) expresses "go to (do something)," as in *menevät nukkumaan*, "they go to sleep."
